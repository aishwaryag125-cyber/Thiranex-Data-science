# Student Performance – Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## 📊 Project Overview

This project performs Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on a Student Performance Dataset to identify patterns, trends, and factors that influence students' final academic performance.

The analysis focuses on understanding the relationships between study habits, attendance, previous academic records, and final grades through statistical summaries and visualizations.

---

## 🎯 Objectives

- Explore the dataset structure and characteristics.
- Analyze student performance distributions.
- Identify relationships between academic and social factors.
- Determine the key variables influencing final grades (G3).
- Visualize correlations and patterns within the dataset.

---

## 🛠️ Technologies Used

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Kaggle Notebook

---

## 📈 Analysis Performed

### 1. Dataset Exploration
- Dataset dimensions and structure
- Data types inspection
- Statistical summary using descriptive statistics

### 2. Distribution Analysis
- Final Grade (G3) Distribution
- Gender Distribution

### 3. Relationship Analysis
- Study Time vs Final Grade
- Absences vs Final Grade
- Internet Access vs Final Grade

### 4. Correlation Analysis
- Correlation Heatmap
- Correlation of features with Final Grade (G3)

### 5. Special Case Investigation
- Analysis of students scoring zero in the final examination

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### Academic Performance Drivers
- Previous grades (G1 and G2) show the strongest positive correlation with the final grade (G3).
- Students with a history of academic failures tend to achieve lower final grades.

### Study Habits
- Study time demonstrates a positive relationship with academic performance.
- Students who dedicate more time to studying generally achieve better results.

### Attendance Impact
- Higher absenteeism is associated with lower academic performance.
- Students with fewer absences are more likely to achieve higher grades.

### Internet Access
- Students with internet access at home tend to perform slightly better on average.

### Demographic Factors
- Gender shows minimal influence on final academic performance in this dataset.

---

## ✅ Conclusion

The analysis reveals that previous academic performance is the strongest indicator of final grades. Study habits and attendance also contribute to student success, while demographic factors have comparatively less influence. These findings highlight the importance of consistent academic engagement and performance throughout the academic year.
